2

Data Thready holds

The inventories below are the system as designed — they are what counsel maps to legal categories, purposes and bases. They make no claim about lawfulness; they state what the product stores and why it exists functionally.

2.1 Account data

what is real today
  • Sign-in accounts with three-tier roles — Root, Account Admin, Standard. Source: ../web/accounts-admin.html · README.md §5 row 13
  • Administrative actions recorded in an append-only audit log. Source: ../web/accounts-admin.html · README.md §5 row 13
  • Per-Account appearance settings (colors, logo, slogan) set by the Root Admin, behind a server-enforced WCAG-AA contrast gate. Source: ../../theming.md §3/§10 · README.md §5 row 14
  • Messenger session credentials the user connects: Telegram sessions are designed-in; the Max adapter is in development and never claimed working. Source: ../web/settings.html · README.md §5 row 3 — [GAP: 5.1]

2.2 Channel content you connect

what is real today
  • Posts from channels the user chooses to follow, including backfilled history with visible progress. Source: ../web/channels.html, ../web/thread-explorer.html · README.md §5 row 2
  • Threads kept as they really are — root posts with organic reply chains, system replies separated and excluded from counts. Source: ../web/thread-explorer.html · README.md §5 row 4
  • The user's direct hashtags, kept distinct from anything the AI derives. Source: ../web/post-detail.html · README.md §5 row 5
  • Media assets with sha256 checksums, renditions and streaming support. Source: ../web/assets-browser.html · README.md §5 row 12

2.3 Processing artifacts

what is real today
  • Derived tags produced by the AI pipeline, always labelled "(derived)" — visibly and in the accessible name. Source: ../web/post-detail.html · README.md §5 row 5
  • Stage outputs of the pipeline: classify, download, convert, analyze, research, reply — with idempotent retry, so nothing is processed twice. Source: ../web/post-detail.html · README.md §5 row 6
  • Research documents carrying source and pass provenance, linked to the source post. Source: ../web/research-viewer.html · README.md §5 row 11
  • Semantic-search embeddings held in a pgvector index. Source: ../../../deployment/index.md §2 · README.md §5 row 25
  • Live processing events streamed over WebSocket/SSE. Source: ../web/events-monitor.html · README.md §5 row 7

2.4 This marketing site

what is real today
  • No third-party CDNs — fonts and assets are self-hosted by design-system contract. Source: ../../design-system.md §4 · README.md §5 row 22
  • The only client-side storage these designed pages use is the theme preference, kept in the browser under localStorage['thready-theme']. Source: ../../theming.md §2 · this page's own head script
[OPERATOR — legal counsel text required]

Cookie and analytics disclosure for the marketing site. Whether analytics exist at all — or their deliberate absence is stated — is an open decision under [OPEN: THREADY-DES-MKT-02]; nothing is documented today, so nothing is claimed here.

[OPERATOR — legal counsel text required]

Legal categorization of the inventories above: data categories, processing purposes, legal bases, special-category handling, and any profiling or automated-decision disclosure counsel deems required for the AI pipeline.

3

Where data is processed

what is real today
  • Thready runs on Helix-operated infrastructure: a single Hetzner dedicated host with three fully-separated environments (dev / sta / prod) as rootless Podman stacks. Source: ../../../deployment/index.md §1 · README.md §5 row 23
  • Secrets are loaded at runtime only and never logged. Source: ../../../deployment/index.md §1 · README.md §5 row 23
[OPERATOR — legal counsel text required]

Hosting jurisdiction statement, subprocessor list (if any), and international-transfer language. The design records the operated-infrastructure facts above; it asserts no legal conclusions about them.

4

Retention and backups

what is real today
  • Daily full backups plus hourly database incrementals, with a documented restore runbook — recovery-point objective about one hour, recovery-time objective about four. Source: ../../../deployment/index.md §1 (Q41/Q45) · README.md §5 row 24
  • Regenerating a research document marks the old document stale rather than silently replacing it. Source: ../web/research-viewer.html · README.md §5 row 11
[OPERATOR — legal counsel text required]

Retention periods per data category, deletion-on-request mechanics, and how deletion interacts with the backup cycle above (backup expiry, restore-window caveats). No retention period is documented in the ground truth, so none is invented here.

Your rights

The designed layout reserves one subsection per right counsel confirms applies — typically access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection, and complaint to a supervisory authority. Which of these apply, and in which jurisdictions, is counsel's determination, not the design's.

[OPERATOR — legal counsel text required]

The rights that apply, how each is exercised, response timelines, identity-verification requirements, and the competent supervisory authority. The design asserts no right and no jurisdiction.
